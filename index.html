Message

Christmas is celebrated on December 25th to commemorate the birth of Jesus Christ. It is a time for family gatherings, gift-giving, and spreading joy and goodwill. Traditions include decorating Christmas trees, singing carols, and enjoying festive meals.


Objective of Craft

Project Description: Eco-Friendly Christmas Tree with Interdisciplinary Learning As part of our Christmas celebration, our class created an eco-friendly Christmas tree using waste and reusable materials. The main aim of this project was to spread awareness about sustainability by practicing the principles of reduce, reuse, and recycle, while also integrating academic learning in a creative way. Different subjects were thoughtfully incorporated into various components of the Christmas tree to make the craft both educational and meaningful. Physics was represented through planets arranged around the tree, symbolizing our learning about the solar system and celestial bodies. Mathematics was integrated using origami decorations, highlighting the use of geometric shapes, symmetry, and precision involved in paper folding. History was depicted through the stable where Jesus was born, helping us understand the historical and cultural significance of Christmas. Biology was creatively shown using a snowman with labeled organs, representing basic knowledge of the human body and its functions. This interdisciplinary approach helped us connect classroom concepts with real-life creativity. The project encouraged teamwork, critical thinking, and artistic expression while reinforcing subject knowledge in an enjoyable and hands-on manner. Overall, this eco-friendly Christmas tree reflects the spirit of Christmas, the importance of environmental responsibility, and the joy of learning across different subjects.
